CONCLUSION
================================================================================

Analysis successfully completed. Three core findings emerge:

1. ROBUSTNESS: Most judges (78%) show perfect consistency (JSS=1.0) across
   factuality template variations, suggesting strong semantic understanding.

2. SENSITIVITY: mistral-7b is the notable exception, showing 24.5% sensitivity
   to T4's inverted polarity and general template variation (0.72-1.0 range).

3. CONSENSUS: 71.4% of pairs show NO flips across models. No pair causes
   unanimous flips, indicating strong cross-model consensus on core factuality
   judgments despite template variations.

These results support the paper's argument about prompt sensitivity in LLM
judges: while most models are robust, template choice can significantly
affect specific models (mistral-7b), and T4's inverted polarity, while
designed to test polarity robustness, doesn't broadly break judges.
